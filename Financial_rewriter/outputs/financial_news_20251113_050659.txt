Tenneco Clean Air raises Rs 1,080 crore via anchor book ahead of Rs 3,600-crore IPO

REWRITTEN (CONVERSATIONAL):
HEADLINE: Tenneco Clean Air's Big IPO Gets ₹1,080 Crore Boost from Investors

Tenneco Clean Air India, a company that makes parts for cars, just got a huge vote of confidence from big investors. They raised ₹1,080 crore from 58 anchor investors just a day before opening their ₹3,600-crore Initial Public Offering (IPO) to the general public. But what does that even mean?

Let's break it down. An IPO is when a company decides to list its shares on the stock market for the first time, allowing regular people like you and me to buy a part of it. Tenneco Clean Air India's IPO is worth ₹3,600 crore, which is a significant amount. To put it into perspective, ₹3,600 crore is roughly the annual budget of a mid-sized Indian city.

Now, anchor investors are typically big institutions or high net worth individuals who invest a large amount of money in an IPO before it opens to the public. In this case, 58 anchor investors put ₹1,080 crore into Tenneco Clean Air India, which is about 30% of the total IPO amount. This is a good sign, as it indicates that these big investors believe in the company's potential.

The IPO will be open from November 12 to November 14, and you can buy shares between ₹378 and ₹397 each. Here's the interesting part: the company itself won't receive any money from this IPO. It's an "offer-for-sale" by the promoter, Tenneco Mauritius Holdings, which means they're selling their existing shares to new investors.

Some big Indian mutual funds, like SBI Mutual Fund, ICICI Prudential MF, and HDFC AMC, have already invested ₹588 crore (54.15% of the anchor portion) in Tenneco Clean Air India. Other investors, like SBI Life Insurance and HDFC Life Insurance, have also jumped in. This kind of support from established investors can be reassuring for individual investors like you and me.

Tenneco Clean Air India is a part of the US-based Tenneco Group, which has a global presence in the automotive industry. As a car owner, you might not directly know about Tenneco, but their products are likely used in your vehicle. They make components like exhaust systems and emission control products.

The fact that big investors are backing Tenneco Clean Air India is a positive sign. However, as with any investment, it's essential to do your research and consider your own financial goals before deciding to invest in the IPO. Will you be investing in Tenneco Clean Air India's IPO?

Lenskart IPO Day 1 Updates: Issue booked 1.13x so far, retail investors steal the show. GMP hints 17% listing pop.

REWRITTEN (CONVERSATIONAL):
HEADLINE: Lenskart IPO Gets a Spectacular Start: 1.13x Subscription on Day 1!

So, you must have heard about Lenskart, right? They're that popular eyewear brand that's been making waves in the Indian market. Well, their Initial Public Offering (IPO) opened on October 31, and it's been a wild ride so far! An IPO is essentially when a company decides to list its shares on the stock market for the first time, allowing people like you and me to buy a part of it.

Let's get to the juicy part: on the first day itself, Lenskart's IPO was subscribed 1.13 times! To put it simply, that means the number of shares people wanted to buy was 1.13 times more than the number of shares available. And guess what? Retail investors (that's you and me, regular people, not big institutions) were the stars of the show, snapping up a significant chunk of the available shares.

Now, you might be wondering what the big deal is. Lenskart is looking to raise ₹2,150 crore through this IPO, and the price band has been set between ₹382 to ₹402 per share. If it lists at the higher end, the company could be valued at a whopping ₹69,700 crore! To give you some context, that's roughly the GDP of a small country.

The IPO includes not just fresh shares but also an Offer for Sale (OFS), where existing shareholders, including promoters like Peyush Bansal and some big investors, will be selling their shares. Think of it like a garage sale, but instead of old furniture, they're selling their Lenskart shares.

So, what's Lenskart planning to do with the money raised? They want to expand their business by opening more company-owned stores across India and covering some of the operational costs. Makes sense, right? They've been growing rapidly since their inception in 2008, and now they're looking to take it to the next level.

The Grey Market Premium (GMP) is another interesting aspect to watch. GMP is like an unofficial indicator of how much people are willing to pay for the shares above the IPO price. Today, Lenskart's GMP is ₹70, which suggests that the shares might list at around 17% above the upper price band. We'll have to wait and see how it plays out.

All in all, Lenskart's IPO is off to a great start, and it's exciting to see how it unfolds in the coming days. Will you be investing in Lenskart? Only time will tell!

Capillary Technologies IPO: Get Ready to Invest at ₹577 per Share!

REWRITTEN (CONVERSATIONAL):
Imagine being able to manage your retail business more efficiently, with software that helps you understand your customers better. That's what Capillary Technologies does - it provides artificial intelligence-based cloud-native Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) products to businesses. Think of SaaS like a Netflix subscription for businesses; instead of buying software outright, companies pay a recurring fee to use it. Now, Capillary Technologies is going public with its Initial Public Offering (IPO), and the price band is set between ₹549-577 per share.

Let's break it down: an IPO is when a private company issues stocks to the public for the first time, allowing it to raise capital and become a publicly traded company. In this case, Capillary Technologies is raising ₹877.5 crore at the upper price band of ₹577 per share. The IPO will be open for subscription from November 14 to November 18. You can bid for a minimum of 25 equity shares, and then in multiples of 25 shares thereafter. Equity shares represent ownership in the company, so when you buy shares, you're essentially buying a tiny piece of Capillary Technologies.

Here's the interesting part: Trudy Holdings, one of the investors, is selling its shares at a whopping 80% premium to its original cost. To understand this, imagine you bought a ₹100 share for ₹50. If you sell it at ₹90, you're making a profit, right? That's roughly what Trudy Holdings is doing, selling its shares at ₹577 when its average cost was around ₹319 per share. The company is offloading 6.88 lakh shares, which is a significant chunk.

The IPO is divided into two parts: a fresh issue of ₹345 crore and an offer-for-sale (OFS) worth ₹532.5 crore. Think of the fresh issue like a new fundraising effort - the company is issuing new shares to raise capital. The OFS, on the other hand, is like a resale - existing shareholders are selling their shares to new investors. In this case, the promoter, Capillary Technologies International, and Trudy Holdings are selling their existing shares.

As an investor, you might be wondering if this is a good opportunity. The company has reserved ₹2 crore worth of shares for its employees at a discount, which is a nice perk. With the IPO price band set, it's now up to you to decide whether to invest in Capillary Technologies. Will you be buying in?
